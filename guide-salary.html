How to negotiate your salary

A few minutes of discomfort can be worth hundreds of thousands over a career.

Negotiating pay is one of the highest-return uses of your time — a single successful negotiation compounds, because future raises and offers often build on your current number. Yet most people skip it out of discomfort.

Before the conversation

  • Know your market — research the range for the role, level, and location.
  • Quantify your value — specific results you've delivered.
  • Decide your target and walk-away in advance.

During

  • Let them name a number first when you can.
  • Anchor to the top of your researched range, backed by your value.
  • Negotiate the whole package — signing bonus, equity, start date, title, flexibility — not just base.
  • Be collaborative, not adversarial; you want to work here.

The math

A $5,000 higher starting salary isn't $5,000 — it's that amount compounded by every future percentage raise, plus higher matches and often higher future offers. Over a career it can total six figures.
