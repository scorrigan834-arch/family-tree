Smart spending and ads

Spending isn't bad — but spending without thinking is how money quietly disappears. As you get older you'll notice that a lot of what makes you "want" something isn't really you at all. It's advertising.

How ads work on you

Companies spend billions to make you feel you need their product — through influencers, catchy videos, limited-time deals, and making it seem like everyone else already has it. Noticing this is a superpower: once you see the trick, it loses its grip.

Smart-spending habits

  • Wait before you buy. Give a want a day or two — many just fade.
  • Compare options and prices instead of grabbing the first thing.
  • Ask: do I actually want this, or was I sold it?
  • Value over hype — the popular brand isn't always the better buy.

Try this: next time you feel a strong urge to buy, pause and spot what created it — an ad, a friend, a countdown timer? Naming it puts you back in charge.
