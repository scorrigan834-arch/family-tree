Needs versus wants

Some things we need to live — food, a home, warm clothes, going to the doctor. Others are wants — toys, treats, the newest version of something. Both are perfectly fine. The skill worth building young is simply telling them apart before deciding.

Make it a game

  • Name an everyday item and have your child call out "need!" or "want!"
  • Dig into the tricky ones: a warm coat is a need — but is this particular coat a want?
  • At the store, pause before a purchase and ask the same question together.

This isn't about saying no to wants. It's about noticing the difference on purpose, so that spending becomes a choice rather than a reflex. Children who can name a want as a want grow into adults who don't confuse the two — no matter how much is in the account.
