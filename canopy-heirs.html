Preparing your heirs

Estate planning prepares your assets. Preparing your heirs is the other, often-neglected half — and studies of family wealth show it matters even more. Money is far more often lost because the next generation wasn't ready than because the plan was flawed.

What preparing heirs means

  • Talk openly, over time, about money, values, and what you hope it's used for — not just what they'll receive.
  • Share the "why" behind the wealth — the work, the values, the purpose.
  • Bring them in gradually — introduce them to your advisors and let them participate before they need to.
  • Give responsibility early, in proportion to readiness, so stewardship is a skill they've practiced.

The families whose wealth lasts treat raising a capable heir as seriously as growing the balance sheet.

For families building or passing on wealth: Consider a family meeting or a written statement of values alongside your estate documents. A good advisor can help facilitate these conversations \u2014 and heirs who understand both the plan and its purpose are dramatically more likely to preserve it.
