Family and insurance

Protecting your money matters as much as growing it. A single emergency shouldn't be able to undo years of careful progress — and the tools for that are insurance and a cash cushion.

The safety net

  • Emergency fund — aim for several months of essential expenses in easy-to-reach savings. Size it with the emergency-fund calculator.
  • Health insurance — a serious medical event is one of the most common causes of financial hardship.
  • Life and disability insurance — essential once other people depend on your income.
  • Umbrella liability — inexpensive extra protection that becomes important as assets grow.

Insurance can feel like paying for nothing — right up until the day it's the only thing standing between your family and a crisis. Review coverage as life changes: a marriage, a child, a home, or a new level of assets are all cues to revisit it.
