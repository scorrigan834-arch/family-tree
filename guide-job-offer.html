How to evaluate a job offer

Salary is one number on a page with a dozen that matter.

A job offer is far more than its salary. Comparing offers — or deciding whether to take one — means weighing the whole package and the trajectory, not just the headline number.

Total compensation

  • Base salary — the guaranteed cash.
  • Bonus — target vs guaranteed, and how it has actually paid out.
  • Equity — stock or options, the vesting schedule, and a clear-eyed view of its real value.
  • Retirement match — a 5% match is a 5% raise most people overlook.
  • Health, PTO, and benefits — real dollar value; strong coverage can be worth thousands.

Beyond the money

  • Growth and learning — will this build skills and open doors?
  • The manager and team — the biggest driver of day-to-day happiness.
  • Flexibility, commute, and stability.

Compare offers on total value and five-year trajectory, not just this year's base.

For families building or passing on wealth: At senior levels the complexity is in equity and deferred compensation — RSUs vs options, vesting cliffs, 83(b) elections, and the concentration risk when much of your net worth rides on one employers stock. These are worth reviewing with an advisor before signing, and again at each vesting event.
