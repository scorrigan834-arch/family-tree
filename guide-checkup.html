Am I on track for my age?

Rough benchmarks to sanity-check your progress — without turning them into anxiety.

It's natural to wonder whether you're "behind." Benchmarks can be a useful sanity check — as long as you treat them as rough guides, not verdicts. Everyone's path, income, and timing differ.

Common savings benchmarks

One widely cited guide suggests aiming to have saved, across retirement and investments, roughly:

  • 1× your salary by age 30
  • 3× by 40
  • 6× by 50
  • 8× by 60
  • 10× by 67

Behind these? You're not alone, and it's fixable — raising your savings rate and letting time compound does remarkable work. Ahead? Great — keep the habits going.

Better questions than "am I behind?"

  • Is my savings rate trending up? (This matters more than any single balance.)
  • Is high-interest debt under control?
  • Do I have an emergency fund and the right insurance?
  • Am I investing consistently, whatever the amount?

Check your net worth once or twice a year to watch the trend — the direction matters more than the number.

For families building or passing on wealth: Past a certain point, salary-multiple benchmarks stop being useful — the better measure is whether your assets can sustain the life you want (often framed as your "number," roughly 25× annual spending for financial independence). At that stage the plan shifts from accumulating to allocating, protecting, and passing on — which is what an advisor helps orchestrate.
