Should I change jobs?

Switching is often the fastest way to raise your pay — but weigh what you'd leave behind.

Changing employers is frequently the fastest route to a meaningful raise; internal raises often lag what the market will pay a new hire. But a move is more than a bigger number — weigh the full picture.

Reasons a move often makes sense

  • A significant pay increase your current employer won't match.
  • Better growth, learning, or trajectory.
  • A healthier environment or manager.

What to check before you leap

  • Unvested equity or match — leaving can forfeit real money; time the move around vesting if you can.
  • Benefits differences — insurance, PTO, retirement.
  • Your 401(k) — roll it over rather than cash it out (cashing out triggers taxes and penalties and kills compounding).
  • Stability of the new role and company.

Job-hopping purely for small bumps can wear thin, but staying somewhere underpaid out of inertia is costly too. Move deliberately, for the whole package.
