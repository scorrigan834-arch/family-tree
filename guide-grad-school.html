Is grad school worth it?

A bigger bet than undergrad — run the numbers before you enroll.

Graduate school can be a career accelerator or an expensive detour. Because it adds cost and years out of the workforce, the bar for "worth it" is higher than for undergrad.

Count the full cost

The real price isn't just tuition — it's tuition plus the income you give up while studying (the opportunity cost). A two-year program can easily cost six figures once foregone salary is included.

When it tends to pay off

  • The degree is required or clearly rewarded in your field (medicine, law, some engineering and finance roles).
  • It comes with funding — assistantships, fellowships, or employer sponsorship dramatically improve the math.
  • You have a specific goal, not just uncertainty about what to do next.

When to pause

If you're going mainly to delay decisions, or the field doesn't reward the credential, the cost is hard to justify. Many employers will pay for relevant graduate education — always check before self-funding.
