How much student debt is too much?

A simple rule to keep loans from following you for decades.

Student loans can be a reasonable investment or a long-term burden — the difference is usually the amount relative to what you'll earn.

The rule of thumb

A widely used guideline: don't borrow more in total than you expect to earn in your first year after graduation. Keep total debt at or below that first-year salary and payments generally stay manageable on a standard payoff. Borrow well beyond it and payments can crowd out saving, investing, and life choices for years.

Borrow smart

  • Exhaust grants and scholarships first — free money.
  • Prefer federal loans before private; they offer income-driven repayment and forgiveness options private loans don't.
  • Only borrow what you need for education, not lifestyle.
  • Understand the repayment plan before you sign.

If you're already carrying loans, the debt-payoff calculator shows how extra payments shorten the timeline.

For families building or passing on wealth: Families sometimes lend to a child for school directly. An intra-family loan at the IRSs low applicable federal rate can beat both private lenders and outright gifting, while teaching repayment discipline. Structured properly — a real note with real payments — it keeps the lesson intact without high-cost commercial debt.
