How banks and interest work

A bank is a safe place to keep money — safer than a drawer, and it can even grow a little while it sits there.

What "interest" means

When you keep money in a savings account, the bank pays you a small amount for letting them hold it. That payment is called interest. It's tiny at first, but over time money can earn money — one of the most powerful ideas in all of finance.

Ask a parent about opening a youth savings account. Watching real interest land, even a few cents, makes the idea click.
