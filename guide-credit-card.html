Using a credit card without getting into debt

Used well, it's a free tool that builds credit. Used poorly, it's the most expensive money you'll borrow.

A credit card is neither good nor bad — it's a tool. Used one way it builds your credit, protects your purchases, and even pays rewards, all for free. Used another, it's often the most expensive debt an ordinary person ever carries.

The one rule that matters

Pay the full statement balance every month. Do that and you never pay interest while keeping all the upside. Carry a balance and APRs above 20% compound against you fast — quietly erasing far more than any rewards add.

Habits that keep you safe

  • Autopay the full balance so you never miss or underpay.
  • Keep utilization low — using a small share of your limit helps your score.
  • Treat it like a debit card — only charge what's already in your budget.
  • Don't chase rewards into spending you wouldn't otherwise do; the math rarely works.

If you already carry a balance, that's the priority: see the debt-payoff calculator for how fast extra payments clear it, and whether a lower-rate option could help meanwhile.
