Your first car

A car is often the first big purchase of your adult life — and one where a few smart choices save you thousands. The trick is to look past the sticker price to the true cost of owning it.

New vs used

A new car loses about 20% of its value in the first year. Buying a reliable 2–4 year-old used car lets someone else absorb that steep drop — usually the best value for your money.

The true cost of a car

  • The purchase — price or monthly payment.
  • Insurance — required, and pricier for young drivers; get quotes before you buy.
  • Fuel, maintenance, and repairs.
  • Registration and taxes.

A common guideline: keep all car costs under about 15–20% of your take-home pay. And if you borrow, a shorter loan on a cheaper car beats a long loan on a flashy one. The new-vs-used guide goes deeper.
