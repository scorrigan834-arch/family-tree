Student loans and credit

Borrowing money means paying to use someone else's money. The price is interest, shown as an APR (annual percentage rate). The higher the APR, the more it costs you.

Two rules that protect you

  • Only borrow what you truly need — especially with student loans. Every dollar borrowed is more than a dollar to pay back.
  • With a first credit card, pay the full balance every month. Do that and you build a strong credit history for free. Carry a balance and the interest works against you fast.

Your credit score is a track record of how reliably you repay. Good habits now make renting, borrowing, and even some jobs easier later.
