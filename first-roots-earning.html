Earning your first dollar

Money comes from doing something helpful. Before kids can save or spend well, it helps to feel where money begins: you do work, and you earn.

Try this at home

  • Pick one or two small jobs a child can own — feeding a pet, watering plants, tidying toys.
  • Agree on a small amount they earn when it's done.
  • Hand it over in coins or bills they can hold. The physical part matters at this age.

The lesson isn't the amount — it's the link between effort and reward. Praise the effort, and let them choose what to do with what they earned.
