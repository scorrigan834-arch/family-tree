Taxes 101

How income tax actually works — brackets, deductions, and why a raise never 'bumps you down.'

Taxes feel intimidating mostly because of a few misunderstood ideas. Clear those up and the rest is just mechanics.

Marginal brackets — the big misconception

The U.S. uses marginal brackets: only the income within each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate. Moving into a higher bracket never lowers your take-home — only the dollars above the threshold are taxed more. Your effective rate (total tax ÷ total income) is always lower than your top bracket.

Deductions vs credits

  • Deductions reduce your taxable income. Most people take the standard deduction rather than itemizing.
  • Credits reduce your tax bill dollar-for-dollar, so they're more powerful than a deduction of the same size.

Withholding and filing

Your employer withholds tax each paycheck based on your W-4. You file a return each year to reconcile: a refund means you over-withheld (an interest-free loan to the government); owing means you under-withheld. Aim to land near zero. Pre-tax 401(k) and HSA contributions lower your taxable income today.

For families building or passing on wealth: At higher incomes, tax planning shifts from filing to strategy — tax-advantaged accounts, tax-loss harvesting, asset location, and charitable giving through donor-advised funds or appreciated stock. These are where a good CPA and advisor pay for themselves many times over; the fundamentals here are the base they build on.
