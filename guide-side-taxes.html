Side income & self-employment taxes

Freelance and gig income is taxed differently. Set money aside before it's a surprise.

A side hustle is exciting until tax season, when self-employment income surprises people who didn't plan for it. The rules aren't hard — they're just different from a W-2 job.

What's different about 1099 income

  • No taxes are withheld, so nothing is set aside automatically — that's on you.
  • You owe self-employment tax (~15.3%) on top of income tax, because you pay both halves of Social Security and Medicare.
  • If you'll owe roughly $1,000+, the IRS expects quarterly estimated payments rather than one April bill.

Stay ahead of it

  • Set aside ~25–30% of side income in a separate account the moment it arrives.
  • Track business expenses — home office, supplies, mileage, software — they reduce taxable profit.
  • Keep clean records; a simple spreadsheet is plenty at first.
For families building or passing on wealth: As self-employment income grows, structure starts to matter — an LLC or S-corp election can change how youre taxed, and self-employed retirement plans (Solo 401(k), SEP-IRA) allow far larger tax-advantaged contributions than a standard IRA. This is the point to bring in a CPA; the savings usually dwarf the fee.

Do this now: open a separate account and route a quarter of every side payment into it. Future-you will be grateful in April.
