Freelancing vs a full-time job

Freedom and upside on one side; stability and benefits on the other. Count both honestly.

Freelancing and full-time employment are two different deals, and the "better" one depends on your finances, temperament, and stage of life. The mistake is comparing only the headline rates.

What full-time quietly provides

  • Steady income and predictable cash flow.
  • Benefits — health insurance, a 401(k) match, paid time off, disability coverage — often worth 20–30% on top of salary.
  • Employer-paid payroll taxes — they cover half of Social Security and Medicare.

What freelancing offers

  • Freedom — schedule, clients, and location.
  • Upside — you can often charge more and scale.
  • Control over your work and taxes (more deductions, but more responsibility).

The number that matters

To match a salary, a freelancer generally needs to charge meaningfully more per hour — often 30–50%+ — to cover benefits, both halves of payroll tax, unpaid time (vacation, sick days, gaps between clients), and self-funded retirement. Price for the whole picture, not just the hours.
