Roth vs Traditional vs HSA: which account?

Same investments, very different tax treatment. Here's how to choose.

Where you invest for retirement can matter as much as how much, because each account type is taxed differently. The investments inside can be identical — the difference is when you pay tax.

The main accounts

  • Traditional 401(k) / IRA — pre-tax now (lowering today's taxable income); taxed when you withdraw. Best when you expect a lower tax rate later.
  • Roth 401(k) / IRA — taxed now, and qualified withdrawals are completely tax-free later, growth included. Best when you expect a higher rate later — powerful for younger savers.
  • HSA — with a qualifying high-deductible plan, the only triple tax-advantaged account: deductible in, tax-free growth, tax-free out for medical costs. After 65 it works much like a Traditional IRA for any purpose.

A simple order of operations

  1. Contribute to the 401(k) up to the full employer match — a guaranteed return.
  2. Max an HSA if you're eligible.
  3. Add a Roth IRA, especially while your tax rate is modest.
  4. Return to the 401(k) for more.
For families building or passing on wealth: At higher incomes the choices get more nuanced — backdoor Roth contributions, mega-backdoor strategies, and tax-location across accounts can add up meaningfully. This is where coordinating with a tax professional and advisor earns its keep; the principles above are the foundation those moves build on.
