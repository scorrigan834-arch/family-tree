Saving for something you want

Saving is patience made visible. Young children feel "now" far more strongly than "later," so the job isn't to lecture about the future — it's to make waiting concrete, rewarding, and even a little exciting.

The clear-jar method

  • Use a see-through jar so the pile is always in view.
  • Tape a picture of the goal to the front — the specific toy, game, or outing.
  • Count together each time money goes in, and say out loud how much is left to go.

Consider a small "parent match" — add a coin for every few they save. It rewards the habit and quietly introduces the most important idea in all of finance: that money set aside can grow. When they finally buy the goal with their own saved money, let the pride land. That feeling — I waited, and it was worth it — is the whole lesson.
