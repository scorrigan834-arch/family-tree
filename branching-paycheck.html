Your first paycheck and taxes

Your first paycheck comes with a surprise: it's smaller than your hourly rate times your hours. That's normal — money is taken out before you ever see it.

Gross vs. net pay

  • Gross pay is what you earned before anything is removed.
  • Net pay (take-home) is what actually lands in your account.
  • The gap is mostly taxes, plus things like health insurance if you have it.

Read your pay stub once, slowly. Knowing where each dollar goes — federal tax, state tax, Social Security — turns a confusing slip of paper into something you understand.
