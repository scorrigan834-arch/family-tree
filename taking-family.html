Money and relationships

As life gets more serious, so does money's role in your relationships. Handled openly, money brings partners closer; avoided, it's one of the top sources of conflict. The whole skill is simply talking about it.

Combining finances

  • Joint, separate, or hybrid — a shared account for joint costs plus personal accounts each partner controls is the most common. There's no single right way, only the one you both agree on.
  • Split fairly — when incomes differ, dividing shared costs by proportion of income often feels fairer than 50/50.
  • Be transparent — hidden debt or accounts erode trust faster than any number.

Talking about it

Set a short, regular money check-in rather than one tense annual argument. Name shared goals — a trip, a home, a family — so budgeting feels like teamwork.

Starting a family adds costs and makes protection matter: revisit your budget, insurance, and a will with guardianship. The couples guide and kids guide go deeper.
