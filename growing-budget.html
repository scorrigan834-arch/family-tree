Making a simple budget

A budget is simply a plan for money before you spend it. Even a modest allowance is easier to handle — and stretches further — with a plan attached to it.

The three-part plan

  • Spend — money for now: small, everyday things.
  • Save — money set aside for something bigger and later.
  • Give — money for a person or cause they care about.

Decide the split before the money arrives — perhaps half to spend, most of the rest to save, a little to give. When the plan comes first, there's never that puzzled feeling of wondering where it all went.

For families building or passing on wealth: The "give" jar is small, but it teaches something advisors spend careers on: that money is a tool for values, not just consumption. Families who talk about generosity early tend to raise heirs who see wealth as a responsibility, and who carry the familys charitable intent forward on purpose.
