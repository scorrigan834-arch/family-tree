How much life insurance do you need?

Term insurance, and a simple way to size it.

Life insurance replaces your income for the people who depend on it. If no one relies on your earnings, you may not need it; if others do, it's essential — and usually cheaper than people expect.

Term vs whole life

  • Term insurance covers a set period (say 20–30 years) at low cost. For the vast majority, this is the right choice — it covers the years your family depends on you.
  • Whole/permanent lasts for life and builds cash value, but costs far more. It has specific uses (below) and is often oversold for typical needs.

How much

A common rule of thumb is 10–12× your annual income. A more tailored method is DIME: add your Debts, Income to replace, Mortgage, and Education costs for your kids. Buy enough term coverage to reach that number.

For families building or passing on wealth: For larger estates, permanent life insurance has real uses beyond income replacement — providing liquidity to pay estate taxes without forcing the sale of a business or property, and equalizing inheritances among heirs. Held in an irrevocable life insurance trust (ILIT), the payout can sit outside the taxable estate. This is specialized planning worth doing with professionals.

Do this: if people depend on your income, price a term policy today — healthy young applicants often pay less than a phone bill for substantial coverage.
