Disability insurance: protecting your income

Your ability to earn is your biggest asset. Almost no one insures it.

Ask someone their most valuable asset and they'll say their home or investments. It's usually neither — it's their ability to earn an income over a working lifetime. Yet disability, which can halt that income, is one of the least-insured risks.

Why it matters

You're statistically far more likely to be unable to work for a stretch during your career than to die during your working years. If a paycheck vanished for months or years, savings alone rarely bridge the gap.

The basics

  • Short-term disability covers weeks to months; long-term covers years and is the more important one.
  • Employer coverage is a great start but is often limited and taxable, and ends if you leave the job.
  • "Own-occupation" policies pay if you can't do your job (not just any job) — stronger protection, worth the cost for specialized careers.
For families building or passing on wealth: The higher your income, the more there is to protect — and group coverage usually caps out well below what high earners actually make. A private own-occupation policy covering a larger share of income is core protection for physicians, executives, and business owners whose family lifestyle depends on that earning power.

Do this: check what disability coverage you already have through work, then consider a supplemental private policy to close the gap — especially early, while it's cheap and you're healthy.
