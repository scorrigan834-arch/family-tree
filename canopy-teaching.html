Teaching your kids about money

The most valuable financial gift you give your children isn't money — it's the ability to handle it. Kids learn money mostly by watching and doing, so the goal is small, age-appropriate lessons over many years, not one big talk.

What works

  • Let them earn, choose, and even make mistakes with small amounts, while the stakes are low.
  • Use real moments — the store, an allowance, a goal they're saving toward.
  • Be open about money decisions at an age-appropriate level; silence teaches that money is scary or secret.
  • Model it — children copy what you do far more than what you say.

The First Roots and Growing stages give you the age-by-age path, and the games make it something you can do together.

For families building or passing on wealth: For families with significant assets, this is the single highest-leverage thing you can do. Research consistently finds that wealth is preserved not by better planning but by better-prepared heirs. Teaching children to steward money \u2014 starting young \u2014 is what keeps a legacy intact.
