Rent or buy a home?

Buying isn't automatically smarter, and renting isn't wasted money. It depends on time and the true costs.

"Renting is throwing money away" is one of the most repeated — and most misleading — pieces of money advice. Both renting and buying have real costs; the right choice depends mostly on how long you'll stay and the full math.

The honest comparison

  • Renting buys flexibility and freedom from maintenance and market risk — valuable if you might move within a few years.
  • Buying can build equity, but the costs run well past the mortgage: down payment, closing costs, property taxes, insurance, and maintenance (budget ~1% of the home's value per year).

The rule of thumb

Buying usually wins only if you'll stay long enough — often five-plus years — to outrun those transaction costs. Below that, renting frequently comes out ahead once everything is counted. Run your own numbers with the rent-vs-buy calculator.

For families building or passing on wealth: For those who can pay cash, the question becomes opportunity cost — a mortgage at a modest rate may be worth keeping if the down payment could earn more invested, especially after the tax treatment of each. And a home is a lifestyle asset, not a core investment; concentrated real estate (a primary residence plus second homes) should be weighed within the whole portfolio.
