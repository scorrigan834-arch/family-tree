What insurance do you actually need?

Insurance is for what you can't afford to replace — not for everything.

Insurance exists to transfer risks you couldn't absorb on your own. The trick is buying coverage that protects against genuine catastrophe — and skipping the rest.

The coverage most people need

  • Health insurance — a serious illness is a top cause of financial ruin. Non-negotiable.
  • Auto insurance — legally required, and liability protects your assets if you cause an accident.
  • Renters or homeowners — covers your home and belongings, and includes liability.
  • Life insurance — essential once someone depends on your income (see how much).
  • Disability insurance — protects your ability to earn (see the guide).

What to usually skip

Extended warranties, credit-life insurance, and narrow policies for small, affordable risks generally cost more than they're worth. Insure the catastrophes; self-insure the small stuff.

For families building or passing on wealth: As assets grow, an umbrella liability policy becomes important — inexpensive extra coverage (often $1–5M) stacked above your auto and home limits that shields your net worth from a major lawsuit. Its one of the highest-value, lowest-cost policies a family with assets can hold.
