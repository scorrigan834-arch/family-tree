Glossary

Quick definitions of the terms you'll meet across the site.

  • Interest — the cost of borrowing money, or the reward for saving it.
  • APR — annual percentage rate; interest shown as a yearly figure.
  • Compound interest — earning interest on your interest, so money grows faster over time.
  • Budget — a plan for your money before you spend it.
  • Gross vs. net pay — what you earn before deductions vs. what actually lands in your account.
  • Credit score — a track record of how reliably you repay what you borrow.
  • Principal — the original amount of a loan or investment, before interest.
  • Net worth — everything you own minus everything you owe.
  • Emergency fund — savings for surprises, usually a few months of expenses.
  • Index fund — an investment that spreads money across a whole market at once.
  • 401(k) — a workplace retirement account, often with an employer match.
  • Roth IRA — a personal retirement account with tax-free growth.
  • Diversification — spreading money across many investments to lower risk.
  • Inflation — the slow rise in prices that shrinks what a dollar buys.
  • Beneficiary — the person you name to receive an account or policy.
