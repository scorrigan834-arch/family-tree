Healthcare and long-term care

Healthcare is one of the largest and most overlooked costs of later life. Planning for it protects both your savings and your family.

The pieces to understand

  • Health insurance now — and an HSA if you qualify, which is triple tax-advantaged and can be saved for future medical costs.
  • Medicare — federal health coverage that begins at 65, but it doesn't cover everything; dental, vision, and most long-term care fall outside it.
  • Long-term care — help with daily living as you age. It's expensive and rarely covered by Medicare, so it deserves a plan.

Ways to prepare

  • Build medical costs into your retirement number — they're larger than most people expect.
  • Consider long-term-care insurance or earmarked savings, ideally in your 50s–60s.
  • Keep an HSA growing if you have one; after 65 it works like a flexible retirement account.
For families building or passing on wealth: For larger estates, healthcare and long-term-care planning is also about protecting what youll pass on \u2014 a multi-year care event can consume significant assets. Self-funding, insurance, and certain trust strategies each have a place; coordinate them with your advisor well before theyre needed.
