Building your emergency fund

Before investing aggressively or chasing big goals, there's one foundation to lay: an emergency fund. It's the cash cushion that keeps a surprise — a job loss, a car repair, a medical bill — from turning into debt.

How much

  • Start with a $1,000 starter to handle small emergencies without a credit card.
  • Build toward 3–6 months of essential expenses.
  • Lean higher if your income is variable or you're the only earner. Size it with the emergency-fund calculator.

Where and how

  • Keep it in a separate high-yield savings account — safe and accessible, not invested.
  • Automate a small transfer each payday, and route windfalls straight in.
  • Once it's funded, shift that money toward investing.

An emergency fund isn't exciting, but it's what lets every other part of your plan survive a bad month.
