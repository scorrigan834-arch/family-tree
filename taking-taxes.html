Understanding your taxes

Taxes feel intimidating, but a few core ideas cover most of what you need as a young earner.

Marginal brackets

Income is taxed in brackets, and only the income within each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate. A raise never lowers your take-home pay — that's a common myth. Your effective rate is always lower than your top bracket.

Deductions, credits, and withholding

  • Deductions lower your taxable income; most people take the standard deduction.
  • Credits cut your tax bill dollar-for-dollar — more powerful than a deduction of the same size.
  • Your W-4 sets how much is withheld each paycheck. A big refund means you over-withheld — an interest-free loan to the government — so aim to land near zero.

Contributing to a 401(k) or HSA lowers your taxable income today. Estimate your take-home with the calculator, and see the Taxes 101 guide to go deeper.
