Renting versus buying

Renting isn't "throwing money away," and buying isn't automatically the smart move. Each fits a different life, and the honest comparison is more than a monthly payment.

Weigh it honestly

  • Renting offers flexibility and no surprise repair bills — a real advantage if you might move within a few years.
  • Buying can build equity over time, but it adds costs first-timers often forget: down payment, closing costs, property taxes, insurance, and ongoing maintenance.

A useful rule of thumb: buying tends to pay off only if you'll stay put for several years — long enough to outrun the upfront costs. Don't rush in because it feels like the expected next step; run the numbers first with the rent-vs-buy calculator, and remember the payment is only part of the picture.
