How much house can I afford?

The bank's maximum and your comfortable maximum are rarely the same number.

Lenders will often approve you for more than you should comfortably spend. "How much can I afford" is really a question about the life you want around the house — not just the biggest loan you can qualify for.

The classic guideline

The 28/36 rule: keep housing costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance) under about 28% of gross income, and all debt payments under about 36%. Staying under these keeps a home from crowding out saving, investing, and everything else.

Count the whole cost

  • Down payment — 20% avoids private mortgage insurance, though lower is possible.
  • Closing costs — typically 2–5% of the price.
  • Ongoing — property taxes, insurance, maintenance (~1%/year), utilities, and possibly HOA fees.

Avoid becoming "house poor" — owning a home so expensive that little is left for anything else. Estimate the payment with the mortgage calculator and pressure-test it against your full budget.

For families building or passing on wealth: Higher-value purchases bring jumbo loans, larger tax and insurance carrying costs, and the choice between financing and paying cash. A bigger house also means bigger everything — furnishing, upkeep, staff — so the sustainable number is about total carrying cost within the broader plan, not just qualifying for the loan.
