Earning money as a teen

Your teens are a great time to start earning your own money. It builds skills, confidence, and a real sense of what money is worth once you've worked for it.

Ways to earn

  • Classic jobs — babysitting, pet-sitting, yard work, tutoring younger kids.
  • A part-time job once you're old enough — retail, food, summer camps.
  • Small ventures — selling crafts, reselling, or a service you're good at.

Think like an entrepreneur

  • Find a need people will pay to solve.
  • Do reliable, quality work — a good reputation brings repeat customers.
  • Track what you earn and spend, even simply.
  • Reinvest a little — better supplies or tools can grow a small venture.

Earning your own money changes how you see it. Suddenly a $30 purchase is "three hours of babysitting" — and that kind of thinking builds strong money habits for life.
