Giving and philanthropy

For many families, giving is where wealth becomes meaning. Beyond writing a check, thoughtful giving can reflect your values, involve your children, and be surprisingly tax-efficient.

Ways to give well

  • Give appreciated investments instead of cash — you avoid capital-gains tax and can deduct the full value.
  • A donor-advised fund lets you contribute (and deduct) now and grant to charities over time.
  • Involve your kids — deciding together where to give is one of the best ways to pass on values.
  • Give regularly, even modestly; consistency matters more than size.

Giving is also a powerful teacher: children who grow up seeing generosity learn that money is a tool for good, not just something to accumulate.

For families building or passing on wealth: At larger scales, philanthropy becomes part of the estate and tax plan \u2014 donor-advised funds, charitable trusts, and family foundations can align giving with legacy. These are worth structuring with your advisor and CPA; the impact, and the values passed on, compound.
