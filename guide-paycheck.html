Your first paycheck, line by line

Why take-home is smaller than you expected — and what every deduction means.

Your first pay stub can be baffling: the number at the bottom is noticeably smaller than your salary divided by pay periods. Here's every line, decoded.

The deductions, explained

  • Federal & state income tax — withheld based on your W-4 and where you live.
  • FICA — Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%). These fund benefits you'll later receive; your employer matches them.
  • Pre-tax benefits — health premiums and any 401(k) or HSA contributions come out before tax, lowering your taxable income.

Gross vs net

Gross is what you earned; net (take-home) is what lands after all of the above. The gap surprises everyone exactly once.

Two moves on day one

  • Set your W-4 so you don't badly over- or under-withhold.
  • Contribute to the 401(k) up to the employer match — pre-tax, and free money.
