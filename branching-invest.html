Start investing early

Here's a secret the wealthy learn early: the biggest advantage in investing isn't picking winners — it's starting young. Money invested in your late teens or early twenties has decades to compound, and that head start is almost impossible to catch up to later.

Why starting now wins

Because of compounding, a small amount invested today can outgrow a much larger amount invested ten years from now. Time does the heavy lifting — see it for yourself with the compound-interest calculator.

How to actually start

  • If you have any earned income from a job, you can open a Roth IRA — a retirement account whose growth comes out completely tax-free later.
  • If your job offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match — it's free money.
  • Keep it simple: a low-cost index fund spreads your money across the whole market instead of betting on one company.
  • Automate a small, regular amount. Consistency beats timing.

You don't need much to begin — even $20 a paycheck builds the habit and starts the clock.

For families building or passing on wealth: A teenager with a summer job has earned income \u2014 which means theyre eligible for a custodial Roth IRA. A parent or grandparent can help fund it (up to what the child earned), giving decades of tax-free growth. Its one of the highest-leverage gifts a family can make, and a natural first money conversation.
